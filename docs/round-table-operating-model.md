# The Round Table Operating Model

Date: 2026-06-27

## One-Line Concept

The Round Table is a private, application-only men's brotherhood and life-command platform where young men build discipline, standards, relationships, fitness, money control, leadership, and high-ROI networks through structured accountability and council-led development.

## Positioning

The Round Table is not a generic support group, not a course library, and not a hype chat. It is a private brotherhood for men who want standards around them while they rebuild or sharpen their lives.

Public hook:

> Build your life command system.

Expanded promise:

> Join a private brotherhood of men committed to discipline, fitness, money, relationships, style, time, business, leadership, and real-world brotherhood.

## Audience

Primary member:

- Male, roughly 18-27 for the founding message.
- Wants to level up but lacks a serious room.
- May be lost, inconsistent, or behind, but is serious about changing.
- Wants direct standards, not soft motivation.
- Lives in the U.S. for launch geography.

Anti-member:

- Victim mindset.
- Broke with no work ethic.
- Dishonest.
- Drama-driven.
- Disrespectful.
- Wants promotion more than brotherhood.
- Wants status without action.

## Brand Standard

The tone should be:

- Warrior discipline.
- Gentleman standards.
- Grounded accountability.
- Values-based and accessible.
- Serious, direct, masculine, premium.

Avoid:

- Fake-alpha language.
- Therapy-speak.
- Generic self-help.
- Medieval costume language.
- Empty motivation.

## Core Identity

Brotherhood is the umbrella pillar. The development pillars sit under it:

- Body.
- Money.
- Style and presence.
- Relationships.
- Time and discipline.
- Business and career.
- Leadership.
- Brotherhood.
- Communication.
- Mental and spiritual discipline, framed through values rather than doctrine.
- High-value networking.

## Offer

Founding offer:

- $25/month.
- Application approval before payment.
- 90-day minimum commitment.
- Month-to-month after the first 90 days.
- Lifetime founding price lock while active.
- First wave: 25 members.
- Controlled founding cap: 50 members.

Future tiers:

- Member.
- Command.
- Council.

Tier logic:

- Member gets the room, rituals, missions, and foundation.
- Command gets deeper curriculum, all pillar access, richer accountability, and stronger progress tools.
- Council gets advanced access, priority interaction, events, and deeper rooms.

## Onboarding

Onboarding should feel like a threshold, not a form.

Sequence:

1. Apply.
2. Manual review by John/council.
3. Approval.
4. Payment and 90-day commitment.
5. Oath ceremony.
6. Diagnostic.
7. Path assignment.
8. First Campaign begins.

Diagnostic should determine the member's primary transformation path:

- The Foundation Path.
- The Body Standard.
- The Command Path.
- The Money Path.
- The Presence Path.
- The Relationship Path.
- The Builder Path.

## First 90 Days

Recommended curriculum name: **The First Campaign**.

Supporting names:

- The Brotherhood Standard: code and culture.
- The Command Path: one path inside the system.
- The 90-Day Standard: plain alternative.

90-day structure:

- Days 1-7: oath, baseline, path assignment, profile, first standards.
- Days 8-30: consistency, time discipline, body baseline, Sunday planning.
- Days 31-60: chosen pillar development, public check-ins, squad or local participation.
- Days 61-90: leadership, proof of change, local connection, next rank or path decision.

## Community Operating System

Weekly:

- Sunday planning.
- Weekly mission.
- Weekly check-in.
- Member wins.
- Pillar discussions.

Monthly:

- Monthly council call.
- Monthly rankings.
- Monthly path review.

Quarterly:

- Quarterly events.
- Quarterly challenge.
- Quarterly recommitment.

Local:

- U.S.-only geographic channels.
- Members can build real in-person relationships through local sections.
- MVP should provide the structure but avoid overpromising safety, event management, or local leadership until proven.

## Rules

At The Round Table, members:

- Show up.
- Tell the truth.
- Respect brothers.
- Drop excuses.
- Kill victim mindset.
- Take action weekly.
- Keep confidence.
- Give direct feedback.
- Receive correction.
- Promote only in designated channels.

Sensitive topics are allowed, but the culture stays action-oriented: honesty, support, problem-solving, and forward movement.

Consequences:

- Warning.
- Removal.

The council can skip warning for severe behavior that threatens the room.
