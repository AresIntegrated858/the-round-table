# The Round Table MVP Design

Date: 2026-06-27

## Approved Direction

The Round Table is a low-ticket, application-only paid brotherhood and private club for young men, roughly 18-27, who are trying to take command of their lives. It should include men who are currently lost but serious about changing, while rejecting victim mindset, dishonesty, disrespect, and broke-with-no-work-ethic behavior.

The first public promise is not generic self-improvement. It is: build your life command system inside a private brotherhood of men who hold standards together.

The product should launch as a responsive web app that feels mobile-native, then expand into downloadable mobile apps. The MVP should live in this repo and preserve the current Expo Router / React Native Web direction.

## Product Shape

The MVP is a private, values-based brotherhood platform with:

- Application approval before payment.
- A $25/month founding price.
- A 90-day minimum commitment, then month-to-month.
- Lifetime founding price lock while active.
- Three modern tiers: Member, Command, Council.
- Council-led authority: John Maciel, Manny Thompson, Chase Grafton, and Erik Sims.
- A public movement feel with a private application gate.
- A structured onboarding diagnostic that assigns each member a branded transformation path.

The brand stays serious, masculine, grounded, premium, and aspirational. It should feel like warrior discipline, gentleman standards, and practical accountability without fake-alpha performance.

## Audience

Primary launch audience:

- Men roughly 18-27.
- Young men trying to level up.
- Broader U.S. men who want discipline, better relationships, fitness, money control, time command, and higher-standard male friendships.
- Men who may be lost, underbuilt, or behind, but are humble and serious.

Anti-member:

- Victim mindset.
- No work ethic.
- Dishonesty.
- Drama.
- Disrespect toward brothers.
- Men who want status without action.
- Men who only want to promote, flex, or consume.

## Core Pillar System

Brotherhood is the umbrella pillar and identity. The platform should organize development under life-command pillars:

- Body.
- Money.
- Style and presence.
- Relationships.
- Time and discipline.
- Business and career.
- Leadership.
- Brotherhood.
- Mental and spiritual discipline, framed as values-based and accessible.
- Communication.
- High-value networking.

Hard tracking applies to the member's chosen transformation path. Soft tracking applies across all major life-command areas so members do not become one-dimensional.

## Transformation Paths

During onboarding, each applicant/member answers diagnostic questions. Approved members are assigned a branded path based on their greatest constraints.

Initial path options:

- **The Foundation Path**: for men who lack structure, discipline, and basic consistency.
- **The Body Standard**: for men whose first visible weakness is fitness, health, energy, or self-respect.
- **The Command Path**: for men who need time, focus, execution, and life organization.
- **The Money Path**: for men who need earning discipline, budgeting, investing basics, and financial responsibility.
- **The Presence Path**: for men who need style, grooming, confidence, communication, and social competence.
- **The Relationship Path**: for men who need stronger standards with women, family, friends, and brotherhood.
- **The Builder Path**: for men focused on business, sales, career growth, and leadership.

The first 90 days should unlock in a cadence that makes progress feel earned:

1. Days 1-7: orientation, oath, baseline, path assignment, first standards.
2. Days 8-30: foundational discipline, Sunday planning, first mission cycle.
3. Days 31-60: focused pillar development, public check-ins, member wins.
4. Days 61-90: leadership, local connection, results review, next rank decision.

## App Information Architecture

The app should feel like a full life-command center, but the MVP must prioritize the loops that protect culture.

Top-level sections:

- **Home / Command Center**: today's standard, current path mission, weekly check-in, rank/progress.
- **Brotherhood Feed**: main public feed for wins, standards, lessons, and accountability.
- **Pillars**: topic subsections for body, money, style, relationships, time, business, leadership, brotherhood, and communication.
- **Local Tables**: U.S.-only geographic sections by state/metro to help members build real in-person networks.
- **Missions**: weekly missions, path work, challenge completions.
- **Library**: lessons, workshops, replays, templates, resources.
- **Rankings**: public progress, member wins, leaderboard, recognition.
- **Profile**: oath status, standards, path, progress, location, tier, rank.

Community structure should support one main brotherhood plus smaller squads or crews. Squads should be created by path, geography, or cohort once volume supports it.

## Community Operating System

Weekly cadence:

- Sunday planning ritual.
- Weekly mission assignment.
- Weekly check-in.
- Public member wins.
- Ongoing pillar discussion and accountability.

Monthly cadence:

- Monthly council call.
- Monthly rankings/progress recognition.
- Monthly path review.

Quarterly cadence:

- Quarterly events.
- Quarterly challenge or campaign.
- Quarterly standards review and recommitment.

Local cadence:

- Local meetups through geographic channels.
- No unsafe or unvetted in-person commitments in MVP. Local channels coordinate; members remain responsible for judgment and safety.

Member time expectation: 1-2 hours per week minimum.

## Rules And Consequences

Core code:

- Show up.
- Tell the truth.
- Respect the brotherhood.
- Drop excuses.
- Kill victim mindset.
- Take action weekly.
- Hold confidence with sensitive topics.
- Give direct feedback without humiliation.
- Receive correction without ego.
- Promote only in designated channels.

Sensitive personal topics are allowed. The culture should not become therapy-speak. The standard is: honesty, support, problem-solving, and forward movement.

Consequences:

1. Warning.
2. Removal.

The council can remove immediately for severe dishonesty, harassment, unsafe behavior, or culture corruption.

## Offer And Pricing

Launch pricing:

- Founding Member: $25/month.
- Application approval required before payment.
- 90-day minimum commitment.
- Month-to-month after 90 days.
- Lifetime $25/month price lock while active.

Tier architecture:

- **Member**: app access, brotherhood feed, selected pillar access, weekly missions, Sunday planning, soft scorecard, local table access.
- **Command**: full pillar access, deeper curriculum, all missions, rankings, replays, squad participation, expanded scorecard, monthly council call access where appropriate.
- **Council**: highest access, council calls, advanced workshops, priority Q&A, deeper rooms, potential event priority.

The founding $25/month can serve as an initial Member rate. Command and Council can be introduced after founding proof, or gated in the MVP as coming-soon/limited upgrades if infrastructure supports it.

## Curriculum Naming Options

Best options for the first 90-day curriculum:

- **The First Campaign**: strongest fit for warrior discipline and progress without sounding like a course.
- **The Command Path**: clearest for life-command positioning.
- **The Brotherhood Standard**: strongest culture signal.
- **The First Seat**: good onboarding identity, less tactical.
- **The 90-Day Standard**: plain and grounded.

Recommendation: use **The First Campaign** as the 90-day onboarding curriculum, with **The Brotherhood Standard** as the code/culture layer and **The Command Path** as one transformation path.

## Build Approach Options

### Option A: Strategy-first docs, then MVP implementation

Pros: aligns vault, app repo, copy, offer, and operating system before code changes.
Cons: slower visible UI progress.

### Option B: UI prototype first, strategy docs after

Pros: gives John something to see quickly.
Cons: high risk of building the wrong architecture or carrying stale pricing/tier decisions.

### Option C: Narrow MVP implementation only

Pros: fastest path to a working app.
Cons: underbuilds the brotherhood system, curriculum, and launch assets.

Recommendation: Option A. The repo already has an app scaffold and prior docs with stale decisions. Normalize the foundation first, then implement cleanly.

## Implementation Boundary

This design intentionally stops before code changes. The next step is an implementation plan for:

1. Updating tier constants and paywall language.
2. Reworking onboarding around application approval, diagnostic, oath, and path assignment.
3. Building the mobile-native command center.
4. Adding pillar and local sections.
5. Adding launch/admin review screens.

The implementation should avoid overwriting existing uncommitted app-screen edits unless they are reviewed first.
